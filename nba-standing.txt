all games</span></li></ul></section><section><p class="t7 text-asphalt">Strength of Schedule:</p><p class="t8 text-asphalt">Looks at the opponents for your remaining games, their winning percentage on the road or at home, and whether or not either team is on the second night of a back to back.</p><p class="t8 my-5">Ties for playoff positions (including division winners) will be broken utilizing the criteria set forth in subparagraph a. below (in the case of ties involving two teams) and subparagraph b. below (in the case of ties involving more than two teams), and the guidelines set forth in subparagraph c. below.</p><section><p class="t7 text-asphalt">a. Two Teams Tied. In the case of a tie in regular-season records involving only two teams, the following criteria will be utilized in the following order:</p><ul class="my-5 text-asphalt" style="list-style-type:disc;list-style-position:inside"><li><span class="t8">(1) Better winning percentage in games against each other.</span></li><li><span class="t8">(2) Division winner (this criterion is applied regardless of whether the tied teams are in the same division).</span></li><li><span class="t8">(3) Better winning percentage against teams in own division (only if tied teams are in same division).</span></li><li><span class="t8">(4) Better winning percentage against teams in own conference.</span></li><li><span class="t8">(5) Better winning percentage against teams eligible for playoffs in own conference (including teams that finished the regular season tied for a playoff position).</span></li><li><span class="t8">(6) Better winning percentage against teams eligible for playoffs in opposite conference (including teams that finished the regular season tied for a playoff position).</span></li><li><span class="t8">(7) Better net result of total points scored less total points allowed against all opponents ("point differential").</span></li></ul></section><section><p class="t7 text-asphalt">b. More Than Two Teams Tied. In the case of a tie in regular-season records involving more than two teams, the following criteria will be utilized in the following order:</p><ul class="my-5 text-asphalt" style="list-style-type:disc;list-style-position:inside"><li><span class="t8">(1) Division winner (this criterion is applied regardless of whether the tied teams are in the same division).</span></li><li><span class="t8">(2) Better winning percentage in all games among the tied teams.</span></li><li><span class="t8">(3) Better winning percentage against teams in own division (only if all tied teams are in same division).</span></li><li><span class="t8">(4) Better winning percentage against teams in own conference.</span></li><li><span class="t8">(5) Better winning percentage against teams eligible for playoffs in own conference (including teams that finished the regular season tied for a playoff position).</span></li><li><span class="t8">(6) Better net result of total points scored less total points allowed against all opponents ("point differential").</span></li></ul></section><section><p class="t7 text-asphalt">c. Guidelines For Applying Tie-Break Criteria. The following guidelines shall be used when applying the above criteria to break ties for playoff positions:</p><ul class="my-5 text-asphalt" style="list-style-type:disc;list-style-position:inside"><li><span class="t8">(1)(a) Ties to determine the division winners must be broken before any other ties.</span></li><li><span class="t8">(b) When a tie must be broken to determine a division winner, the results of the tie-break shall be used to determine only the division winner, and not for any other purpose.</span></li><li><span class="t8">(2) If a tie involves more than two teams, the tie-break criteria in subparagraph b. shall be applied in the order set forth therein until the first to occur of the following:</span></li><li><span class="t8">(a) Each of the tied teams has a different winning percentage or point differential under the applicable tie-break criterion (a complete breaking of the tie).
